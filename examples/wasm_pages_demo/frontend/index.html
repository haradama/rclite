Drag the sliders, switch waveforms, or hit ✎ draw input and sketch directly on the scope — the magenta trace is the ESN's one-step-ahead forecast, drawn where the signal is predicted to go next.

Here the loop is closed in JavaScript: each ESN output is fed back as the next input, so the network autonomously regenerates the waveform it learned. ⚡ perturb kicks the reservoir state; watch it recover.

Pick a shape or hit ✎ draw a curve and sketch one across the scope. The reservoir pools its state over the whole window (mean aggregation) and names the curve — the bars on the right are the softmax class probabilities updating live.

A signal scrolls across the scope and every timestep is labelled rising or falling — whether the value is higher or lower than it was a few steps ago. That needs short-term memory, which the reservoir supplies. The coloured band under the trace is the per-step class; drive the signal or ✎ draw input and watch it flip.

Built with rclite · models trained in Python, quantization-free f32 kernels cross-compiled to wasm32 (SIMD128) and linked as reactor modules exporting rc_predict. Regression overlays the forecast; classification recovers labels with argmax / softmax over the readout logits. View the build script.
